Learning Outcomes:

  • Grasping the trade-offs between risk and return for various investments.
  • Understanding key investment theories like CAPM, efficient frontier, and strategies in hedge funds, mutual funds, and ETFs.
  • Studying roles and conflicts in banking, insurance, and fund management.
  • Exploring financial instruments such as derivatives, options, swaps, and futures.
  • Differentiating between exchange-traded and over-the-counter markets, including concepts like securitization.
  • Examining the 2007-2008 financial crisis, its causes, and lessons learned.

2.1 Risk vs. Return for Investors and the Efficient Frontier

  • How to quantify expected return of an individual investment and of a portfolio of investments?
  • How to quantify risk of an individual investment and of a portfolio of investments?
  • What is the theoretical trade-off between risk and expected return in investment?
  • What is the concept of the efficient frontier in investment?
  • How does the introduction of a risk-free asset alter it?
  • How does this lead to the notion of a “market portfolio”?

2.2 The Capital Asset Pricing Model

  • What is the CAPM model, and how is it defined?
  • What is the systematic and nonsystematic risk, and where can you find it in the CAPM model?
  • What is represented by \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) in the CAPM model?
  • Suppose that the risk-free rate is 5% and the return on the market portfolio is 10%. What are the expected returns of investments with \(\beta\) 0, 0.5, and 1.2?
  • What are the theoretical assumptions of the CAPM model? Is this model still useful in the real world?
  • How is it possible to achieve positive \(\alpha\), and how much is the weighted average \(\alpha\) of all investors?
  • How does arbitrage pricing theory differ from the CAPM model?

2.3 Risk vs. Return for Companies

  • How should a company decide whether the expected return on a new investment project is sufficient compensation for its risks?
  • What are the bankruptcy costs, and how does it influence the behavior of investors?
  • How is the stable financial system secured?
  • What is the difference between risk decomposition and risk aggregation?
  • What is a credit rating? Who provides credit rating? What is the best and the worst rating?
